About

With a background in psycholinguistics and neurolinguistics, I have been keenly interested and actively researching various aspects of human cognition, particularly with regards to spoken and written language processing, (a)typical language and cognitive development across the lifespan, and individual differences therein.

I hold a PhD in Theoretical and Applied Linguistics from the University of Cambridge, where I conducted psycholinguistic research into predictive reading comprehension in first and second language speakers. For my MSc at University College London, I investigated the linguistic and cognitive profile of aphasia through narrative discourse assessments.

Among my research interests are: psycholinguistics; multilingualism; cognition; neuropsychology; developmental and acquired neurocognitive conditions; speech, language & communication therapy
